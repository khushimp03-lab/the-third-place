❦

first, the problem

Home is the first place. Work is the second.
Where is the third?

Cafés want your table back in forty minutes. Malls want your wallet. Parks shut at sunset and depend on the weather. We’ve quietly run out of places to just be — alone or with people, for hours, doing something with our hands.

The third place is the one we’ve lost. We’re building one back. Come in your pyjamas. Come with your family, friends, or just alone. Pick a room. Stay as long as you like.

❦

the idea

A house. Eight rooms. Eight worlds.

Every room has its own light, music, smell, and reason to be there. The library is hushed. The music room hums with vinyl. The sculpture room is dusty with clay. Worlds you can step into, hour after hour.

  • ✦ a different aesthetic in every room
  • ✦ rooms evolve with the seasons and the trends
  • ✦ stay for ten minutes or six hours
  • ✦ alone is welcome. so is everyone you brought

the rooms

Pick a door. Open it.

◈

the sculpture room

Try your hand at sculpture. Start by moulding clay, move on to learning how to create and build — make your own pieces and take them home with you.

❦

the woodwork room

Experiment and learn how to make things with wood. Get guidance when you want it, or feel free to experiment however you wish.

✻

the parlour room

Philosophy and tea, anyone? Take some time off to read about philosophy, drink tea, have some snacks — go back to the Renaissance and engage in wordplay.

❀

the library

Plain old book borrowing — but a place where you can sit the whole day and finish your book. Or two.

✺

the art room

Never painted in your life, or already an expert? We have room for both. Paint on our canvas, or make your own piece. Experiment with crayons, paints, glass painting, fabric painting — anything your mind pleases.

❋

the craft room

Come crochet, knit, quill, play with loom bands, or make bracelets — anything you wish.

♪

the music room

Go through vinyl records, play them on our turntable, and relax.

✷

the dynamic room

A room that changes with the times — the person, movie, or book of the week. A pop-culture moment to step into for a fortnight, then become something else.

how it works

Walk in. Pick a room. Stay.

i.

drop in, explore, learn

Walk in, sign in, pick a room. We want you to expand your horizon, learn new things, and step outside your comfort zone.

ii.

help, if you need it

Don’t understand how to do something? We’re there to help you. Want to explore and figure it out for yourself? Feel free to do so.

iii.

materials at cost

Clay, wool, paint, canvas, wood — pay for what you take home. No inflated costs, no hidden charges.

iv.

want to volunteer?

We’re always looking for help. Come spend an evening with us, host a room, and help build a community.
